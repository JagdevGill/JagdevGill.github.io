About Me

Hello! I’m Jack Gill, a student-athlete in my third year at McGill working on my Major in Mathematics and Biology. I have long been fascinated by the intersection of Mathematics and Biology and the potential for mathematical methods to enhance our understanding of complex biological systems. My passion lies in Neurology—a field I connect with personally as someone with cerebral palsy. This connection drives my commitment to exploring the intricacies of the brain and nervous system and finding ways to improve the lives of individuals with neurological disabilities.

Outside of academics, I am a member of Canada’s National Para Swimming team who recently had the honor of representing Canada at the 2023 Para Pan American Games where I earned a bronze medal in the 100m Freestyle. Through this digital portfolio, I aim to share my passion for life sciences, my journey as a student athlete, and my desire to contribute to improving the lives of people with disabilities through innovative treatments and unique perspectives. Thank you for visiting – welcome to my digital portfolio!

Education

Currently in my third year at McGill University pursuing a B.Sc in Mathematics and Biology with a specialized focus in Applied Mathematics and Neuroscience streams. My coursework spans Organic Chemistry, Physical Molecular and Cell Biology, Ordinary Differential Equations, Calculus, Real Analysis and Python, providing me with a solid foundation in both theoretical and computational methods.

Swimming

Athlete on Canada’s National Para-swimming Team and a varsity athlete on the McGill Swim Team. Competed internationally at the 2022 Madeira World Para Swimming Championships and the 2023 Santiago Para Pan American games representing Canada. At the 2023 Para Pan American games, I won a bronze medal in the 100m freestyle. Beyond the podium, I assumed a leadership role as the representative for my coaching group addressing the team’s concerns and fostering effective communication between coaches and athletes.
